The PCG (Permuted Congruential Generator) family of RNGs [1] combines a linear
congruential generator (LCG) with a permutation function that scrambles the
output to mitigate the statistical weaknesses inherent in basic LCGs.
Randompack uses the DXSM output permutation, which provides strong scrambling at
moderate additional cost. This variant was adopted by NumPy in 2019 for its
default 64-bit PCG generator. It is also available in several other projects
including the programming languages Rust and Julia.
